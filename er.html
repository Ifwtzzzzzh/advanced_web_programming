01 Business Description & Economic Moat

TAPG is Indonesia’s third-largest listed palm oil group, operating 132,813 ha of nucleus plantations across Kalimantan and Jambi, with 23 mills, a rubber segment, and strategic joint ventures producing ~953,000 tons of CPO annually (including associates). Revenue is 99.8% palm-oil derived; rubber contributes a marginal 0.2%.

C

Cost Leadership

161,000 ha of planted nucleus + plasma, vertically integrated from plantation to mill. Energy self-sufficiency via palm shell, fiber, and biogas (renewable energy +14% YoY in 2025) structurally lowers processing costs vs grid-dependent peers.

S

Quasi-Switching Costs

~11,945 scheme smallholders + ~1,565 independent smallholders embedded in supply chain. Plasma farmer relationships are contractual and multi-year, creating structural supply lock-in.

R

Regulatory / Certification Moat

14 ISPO + 3 RSPO certifications create a de facto licensing barrier for export market access. New entrants require 3–5 years to replicate this, especially under the EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR) traceability requirements.

L

Land Asset Moat

132,813 ha planted nucleus in Kalimantan and Jambi. No new peatland planting since 2016 + Presidential Moratorium means this land bank is practically irreplaceable under current Indonesian regulations.

△ Contrarian View #1 — JV Income as Hidden Annuity

TAPG’s Rp1.07T equity-accounted JV income (2025) represents 23% of pre-tax profit and has grown at a 32% CAGR from Rp349B in 2021. The market prices this as a commodity-correlated stream, but the JV structure (Coldspace PTE and Indo-Agri affiliates) creates a quasi-contractual dividend floor. At current prices, the market is implicitly assigning a ~3× P/E to this income stream. Applying a 6× multiple to JV income alone implies Rp321/share of hidden value.

Capital structure & deleveraging
⚠ Red Flag — Supra-100% Dividend Payout (2024)

In FY2024, dividends of Rp3.315T were paid against net profit of Rp3.120T (payout = 106%). Funded partly by drawing cash reserves and increasing short-term bank loans from Rp75B to Rp620B. This signals Triputra Group is extracting maximum liquidity, limiting organic reinvestment. In FY2025 payout normalised to 88.5%, but remains structurally high.

⚠ Red Flag — Recurring Tax Disputes

Multiple subsidiaries (BBB, GBSM, AMP) have ongoing tax assessments and penalties across FY2021–2025. "Other Operating Expenses" spiked to Rp165B in FY2025 vs Rp41B in FY2024, partly driven by tax penalties. This is a structural governance cost, not a one-off, and creates earnings-quality risk for DCF modelling.

△ Contrarian View #2 — EUDR Compliance as Upside Catalyst

The market assigns a discount to Indonesian palm oil for ESG/regulatory risk. However, TAPG’s peatland policy, 26,446 ha conservation area, and 100% traceability roadmap (by 2026) position it as Best-in-Class for EUDR. If EUDR enforcement creates market consolidation that squeezes non-compliant producers, TAPG could realise CPO ASP premiums of USD 20–40/ton. This is optionality the market prices as a risk, not a catalyst.

△ Contrarian View #3 — WACC Compression Not Priced In

TAPG’s SPOTT ranking improvement and dual KEHATI index inclusion are being underweighted by the market. As Indonesian OJK mandatory ESG reporting tightens (POJK 51), TAPG’s compliance head-start should compress its cost of debt by ~30–50bps relative to non-certified peers within 2–3 years. A 50bps WACC reduction from 11.0% to 10.5% raises DCF intrinsic value by ~8–10%.
